05 Vendor & Operator Progress

2025–2026 saw AI-RAN move from lab to live commercial trials. Handover-specific AI is mostly embedded within broader "AI-native RAN" and mobility portfolios. Each vendor below is treated separately, with its own source links at the end of the subsection. No major vendor sells a standalone "AI handover" product; handover intelligence is bundled into (a) LTM/mobility features in 5G-Advanced software and (b) AI-RAN / RIC automation platforms.

How to read "handover relevance." Some items below are direct (LTM, mobility optimization, beam-tracking for mobility). Others are adjacent — AI-RAN substrates (real-time neural inference, intelligent beam steering, autonomous-network engines) that AI handover decisions run on top of. Both are flagged honestly per vendor.

5.1  Ericsson Sweden Direct

Ericsson leads LTM standardization and frames the roadmap as "zero-latency handovers on the path to 6G," where AI anticipates a user's movement to hand over before signal degradation. It has launched an AI-native / AI-first RAN portfolio (2025–2026) and applies reinforcement learning to RAN algorithms (e.g., AI-based antenna tilting). With T-Mobile US it ran a world-first large-scale commercial trial of an AI-native Scheduler with Link Adaptation on live 5G-Advanced traffic (May 2026): ~10% spectral-efficiency and up to 15% downlink-throughput gains — the real-time-inference substrate future AI handover will use.

Sources: 5G-Advanced handover: LTM · Toward zero-latency handovers (Dec 2025) · Leveraging AI for RAN optimization · The RAN gets smarter (Jun 2026) · T-Mobile + Ericsson AI-RAN world-first (May 2026)

5.2  Nokia Finland Adjacent

Nokia is pursuing a distinct AI-RAN strategy (notably diverging from Ericsson's), centered on RAN automation and applying AI to mobility robustness, parameter optimization, and energy efficiency rather than a single named handover product. Its mobility intelligence surfaces through self-organizing/automation tooling and O-RAN-aligned RIC apps.

Sources: Light Reading — Ericsson & Nokia diverging on AI-RAN · TelecomLead — AI-first RAN & vendor share 2025

5.3  Samsung South Korea Direct

Samsung is active in 3GPP AI/ML-for-RAN ("First Light of the AI/ML-Empowered RAN") and is the most prolific filer of explicit AI-handover patents, covering UE-side and base-station-side inference, ML-based measurement triggers (e.g., "Event A7"), and model life-cycle management (see §8).

Sources: Samsung Research — AI/ML-Empowered RAN in 3GPP · US20250168716A1 — AI-based handover · US20220286927A1 — ML handover management

5.4  Qualcomm & MediaTek USA / Taiwan Adjacent

The leading modem vendors focus on device-side (UE) AI: on-device inference for beam and channel prediction and chipset support for the ML models a UE-side handover decision would run. Qualcomm is also a top-tier 5G SEP holder, giving it strong influence over AI/ML air-interface features that mobility relies on.

Sources: IEEE ComSoc — 5G SEP race (Qualcomm leadership) · AI/ML for Beam Management in 5G-Advanced (standardization perspective)

5.5  NVIDIA & the AI-RAN Alliance Ecosystem Adjacent (platform)

NVIDIA and the AI-RAN Alliance provide the compute platform and 6G AI-native agenda (aligned with 3GPP and O-RAN) on which mobility/handover ML models are trained and executed — the enabling infrastructure rather than a handover feature itself.

Sources: NVIDIA — Boosting AI-driven innovation in 6G with the AI-RAN Alliance, 3GPP & O-RAN
