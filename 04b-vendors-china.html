05b Chinese Vendors & Operators

Chinese players frame AI handover inside autonomous networks (L4) and 5.5G / 5G-Advanced. Each company below is a separate subsection with its own source links.

5.6  Huawei China Direct

At MWC 2025 (Mar 2025) Huawei launched AI-centric 5.5G (5G-Advanced / 5G-A) solutions to "ignite the mobile AI era," including intent-driven products GigaGear (multi-domain — time/frequency/space/power — resource scheduling for diverse users and AI scenarios), GreenPulse (multi-agent energy/spectral efficiency), and GainLeap built on the RAN Intelligent Service Engine (RISE). Huawei is driving Autonomous Network L3 → L4+ and is the largest 5G standard-essential-patent holder. With China Mobile it built a wireless autonomous network whose "Eyes/Brain/Hands" architecture automatically steers antenna beams toward congested areas — direct mobility/coverage optimization (see §5.8 for measured results).

Sources: Huawei — AI-Centric 5.5G Solutions (Mar 2025) · Huawei — 5G-A + AI at MWC · Huawei — 5G-A & scenario-based AI (Jun 2025) · RCR Wireless — Huawei on 5G-A & AI · IEEE ComSoc — 5G SEP race

5.7  ZTE China Direct

At MWC Shanghai 2025 (Jun 2025) ZTE unveiled AIR RAN — legacy RAN deeply integrated with AI via the BBU-embedded AIREngine computing board — plus a Smart Beam-Tracking Unit and M-MIMO with adaptive beam adjustment raising capacity up to 20% (both directly relevant to mobility and beam handover). Its uSmartNet autonomous-network platform underpins deployments such as an AIS (Thailand) build. ZTE also markets AIR RAN / AgentGuard for the mobile-AI era and an AI-driven 5G service platform.

Sources: ZTE — RAN solutions at MWC Shanghai 2025 · RCR Wireless — ZTE AIR RAN & AgentGuard · ZTE — AIS + uSmartNet autonomous network · IEEE ComSoc — ZTE AI infrastructure

5.8  China Mobile China · Operator Direct

China Mobile is pushing an "AI+Network" strategy toward L4 autonomous networks (unveiled at MWC 2025), partnering with Huawei on a wireless autonomous network. Its "Eyes/Brain/Hands" system detects traffic congestion and automatically adjusts antenna beam points toward congested areas — proactive RAN/mobility optimization. Measured results in the Liaoning subnet include VIP-complaint resolution cut from 2 days to 1.5 hours, first-call resolution up from 80% to 96%, 40% less on-site maintenance, 30% lower O&M cost, and for users 45% lower gaming latency / 50% faster uploads. China Mobile is also a leading force in China's 5G-Advanced rollout that is staged as the on-ramp to AI-native 6G.

Sources: Telecoms.com — China Mobile toward AI-powered L4 autonomous network · Telecoms Tech News — China 5G-Advanced & AI-native 6G · SASAC — China Mobile breakthroughs (14th Five-Year Plan)

5.9  China Telecom (with ZTE) China · Operator Direct (vehicular mobility)

China Telecom and ZTE built a "Factory-to-User" 5G-Advanced solution for intelligent connected vehicles featuring intelligent dynamic scheduling that senses traffic-demand changes, and the "industry's first nationwide multi-DNN customized private network + LBO routing" — a wide-area mobility architecture where ~26.7% of in-vehicle traffic breaks out locally to cut latency for intelligent driving. The platform spans 31 provinces and ~1.4 million vehicle accesses (200 vehicles OTA-updated concurrently within 1 hour).

Sources: The Register — 5G intelligent mobility by China Telecom & ZTE (Nov 2025) · ZTE — intelligent connectivity RAN solutions

China vendor snapshot. The Chinese ecosystem frames AI handover inside autonomous networks (L4) and 5.5G / 5G-A: Huawei (GigaGear/RISE + beam steering with China Mobile), ZTE (AIR RAN + Smart Beam-Tracking + uSmartNet), and operators China Mobile ("AI+Network", beam-to-congestion) and China Telecom (vehicular intelligent mobility). The most handover-direct capabilities are AI-driven beam tracking/steering and intelligent mobility for high-speed vehicles; much of the rest is autonomous-network optimization that mobility management sits within.
