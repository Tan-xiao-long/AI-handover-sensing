Other reported results across the literature

  • Deep RL parameter optimization (DDPG): autonomous tuning of hysteresis/TTT reduces handover failures and ping-pong vs. fixed 3GPP settings across varied speeds. [11]
  • LSTM-aided digital-twin RL (UDN): safe offline pre-training then online adaptation improves handover parameter adaptation in ultra-dense networks. [10]
  • Prediction-based CHO for mmWave (deep learning): anticipating link quality reduces handover failures in volatile mmWave links. [15]
  • AI/ML beam management (RAN1 evaluations): temporal/spatial beam prediction cuts measurement overhead while maintaining beam quality — foundational for high-frequency mobility. [12]
Caveat. Most quantitative AI-handover gains come from simulation (ns-3, system-level) under specific scenarios; absolute numbers vary with mobility model, density, and reward design. Standardized LTM interruption figures and the T-Mobile/Ericsson trial gains are the most deployment-grounded data points here.

09 Outlook & Open Challenges

Where it's heading

  • Convergence of LTM + AI: LTM provides near-zero interruption; AI provides the predictive "when/where." Together → "invisible," proactive handover in 6G.
  • AI-native RAN: real-time inference in the RAN (proven at commercial scale in 2026) becomes the platform for per-UE handover control.
  • Cross-domain mobility: unified handover across terrestrial & non-terrestrial (satellite) networks.
  • Standardized model life-cycle: 3GPP/O-RAN converging on data collection, training, inference, and monitoring interfaces.

Open challenges

  • Generalization: models trained in one deployment/mobility regime may not transfer; real-world datasets (e.g., [28]) are scarce.
  • Explainability & safety: RL policies must avoid catastrophic handover failures; digital-twin pre-training helps.
  • UE energy & compute for on-device inference; signaling overhead of ML config.
  • Interoperability: multi-vendor model exchange, and privacy for UE trajectory data (→ federated learning).
