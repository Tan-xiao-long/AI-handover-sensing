08 Patent Landscape

AI/ML-for-handover is an active filing area. Samsung is notably prolific with explicit AI-handover claims; Huawei, Qualcomm, and Ericsson dominate the broader 5G SEP race that these build upon.

PublicationAssigneeDatesCore claim
US20250168716A1 — AI-based handover procedure [20]SamsungFiled Nov 2024; pub. May 2025UE-side and BS-side AI models predict optimal handover timing/target; enhanced measurement report suggests target; ML life-cycle management on HO success.

US20220294666A1 — AI/ML for channel estimation & mobility enhancements [29]SamsungFiled Mar 2022; pub. Sep 2022ML for UL channel prediction, DL channel estimation, and ML-assisted cell (re)selection / handover; bidirectional config + federated updates.

WO2024010399A1 — AI/ML model management &/or training [31](see record)2024Model management/training framework applicable to RAN mobility inference.

Common claimed techniques

  • New ML-inference-based measurement events replacing static thresholds (e.g., "Event A7").
  • UE-side vs. network-side inference split; UE assistance info (velocity, location, trajectory).
  • RRC/MAC signaling to configure, enable/disable, and update ML models.
  • Federated learning & model life-cycle management (monitoring, retraining on HO outcomes).

Broader 5G SEP context

In the ~$15B 5G licensing market, Huawei, Qualcomm, Samsung, and Ericsson lead the standard-essential-patent race — the same firms shaping 3GPP AI/ML mobility work, so their SEP portfolios increasingly encompass AI-native RAN features that handover intelligence relies on. [22]

Note: patent filing ≠ deployment; several explicit AI-handover applications are pending or (in one case above) abandoned. Treat as a signal of R&D direction.
