01 Executive Summary

Handover — moving an active connection from one cell/beam to another as a device moves — is being reshaped by two convergent forces: lower-layer (L1/L2) triggered mobility (LTM) that slashes interruption time, and AI/ML that turns handover from a reactive, threshold-based decision into a predictive, proactive one.

Key takeaway. Standardized "AI handover" today is really two tracks running in parallel. (1) Structural low-latency handover (LTM) is fully specified and being commercialized — AI is not required for it but is the roadmap. (2) AI/ML mobility optimization (measurement/trajectory prediction, RL-based parameter tuning, proactive target selection) is partly standardized (3GPP TR 37.817 framework, Rel-19 study/normative items) and heavily active in research, vendor labs, O-RAN RIC apps, and patents — with 6G positioned as the point where prediction becomes native.

02 Background: Why Handover Needs AI

In legacy 5G NR (Rel-15) mobility, the UE reports Layer-3 filtered measurements (RSRP/RSRQ/SINR) of serving and neighbor cells; when a configured event (e.g., A3) fires, the network issues an RRC reconfiguration to hand the UE to a target cell. This L3 handover is robust but slow: reconfiguration and random access cause a connection interruption that can reach 50–90 ms, with significant signaling overhead. That is unacceptable for latency-critical XR, cloud gaming, remote control, and industrial automation. [1]

Two problems compound this in dense and high-frequency (mmWave) deployments: ping-pong (repeated back-and-forth handovers wasting signaling and hurting throughput) and handover failure / radio-link failure from mistimed or late handovers. Fixed thresholds and hand-tuned hysteresis/time-to-trigger cannot adapt to the huge diversity of speeds, trajectories, and load conditions. This is precisely the regime where machine learning — predicting signal evolution, UE trajectory, and load — offers gains. [8][12]

The two complementary levers

  • Latency lever — LTM: Move the handover trigger and execution down to L1/L2 with pre-configured candidate cells and pre-synchronization, cutting interruption to ~20–30 ms (and toward zero). Structural, deterministic.
  • Intelligence lever — AI/ML: Predict when and where to hand over (measurement/beam/trajectory prediction), and how to set parameters (RL tuning), reducing failures and ping-pong. Data-driven, adaptive.

Core KPIs handover research targets

  • Handover interruption time (ms of data-plane outage)
  • Handover failure rate (HOF) & radio-link failures
  • Ping-pong rate (unnecessary handovers)
  • Signaling overhead per successful handover
  • Throughput / spectral efficiency during mobility
